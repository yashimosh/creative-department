# Strategy — Meridian (example brand)

This is a short strategy file for a fictional editorial brand. It exists to show the pattern. When you fork this client as a starting point, replace it with your real strategy — which will almost certainly be longer and more specific.

## Lane

Independent editorial. Long-form essays on typography, translation, and publication history. The audience is close readers — people who find a 5000-word essay on kerning compelling.

## Priority stack

1. **Publish one long-form essay per month.** No exceptions. Absence is the signature; skipping a month would break it.
2. **Maintain a consistent voice across the archive.** The voice is the brand. Drift kills recognition.
3. **Build a small loyal readership.** 500 readers who open every essay > 50,000 who skim one.

## What this rules out

- Daily / weekly long-form publishing (not sustainable at the quality floor)
- Growth tactics (waitlists, referral bonuses, "share to unlock")
- Cross-posting every essay to every social platform
- Writing about what's trending — editorial voice is counter-cyclical

## The one compressed move

Publish one essay a month, on time, at the quality floor. Everything else (newsletter notes, social index, community, eventually a print edition) follows from that.

## Distribution

- **Primary:** RSS + email subscription from the website
- **Social:** one monthly index post on one platform
- **Partnerships:** occasional cross-publishing with aligned small editorials (one per year at most)

## Horizon

This is a 3–5 year build. If monthly cadence holds for 36 months, the archive becomes a resource. Before 36 months, it's a project.

## When to re-read this

- When tempted to ship twice in a month
- When tempted to skip a month
- When tempted to write about what's trending
- When tempted to add a second newsletter or a podcast
