## Visual DNA — The Signature Elements

Elements that make this brand instantly recognizable, traced back to their philosophical roots.

### Color Rationale
[Why these specific colors? What do they reference? e.g. "The deep navy (#1A1A2E) references the ink of traditional calligraphy manuscripts — connecting to the brand's roots in scholarly communication. The accent red (#E94560) is borrowed from the vermillion used in marginal annotations in medieval Persian texts."]

### Typography Rationale
[Why these typefaces? What do they communicate structurally? e.g. "Playfair Display's high-contrast strokes reference the Didot tradition — Enlightenment-era rationalism. Paired with Inter for body text, the contrast says: ideas are grand, but communication is clear."]

### Spatial Rationale
[Why this approach to space, density, and layout? e.g. "Generous whitespace comes from Japanese Ma (間) — space is not empty, it is active. The grid system comes from Swiss International Style. Together they create a feeling of calm authority."]

### Imagery Rationale
[Why this photographic or illustrative style? What visual tradition does it reference? e.g. "High-contrast, desaturated photography references documentary journalism — truth over beauty. Cropping is tight and editorial, never centered stock-photo framing."]
